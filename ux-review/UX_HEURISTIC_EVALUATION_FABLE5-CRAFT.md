# UX Heuristic Evaluation — The Wharf WOD Timer
**Reviewer:** FABLE5-CRAFT (independent cold review; primary lens: visual design craft and look-and-feel)
**Inputs:** REVIEW_REQUEST.md + the PNGs in `screens/`. Note: the brief lists 21 shots but `17_active_tabata_phase_preview.png` is absent from `screens/` (only `17_active_tabata_complete.png` exists), so the "WORK in Ns" phase-preview state is **unevaluated**. All other screens (01–21) were reviewed.

## 1. Top 5 problems, ranked

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Critical | 10–16, 13 | Stop (red square) ends the workout in a single tap, and no confirmation step is evidenced anywhere; per the capture note, Stop always lands on "Finished!" (13). It sits ~5 cm from Pause, the button the athlete reaches for in a panic. | The target user pauses with sweaty hands at 170 bpm. One mis-tap left of Pause and a 20-minute AMRAP is dead with no resume, no undo, and the screen celebrates it ("Finished!", green check, 100% green bar at 0:19 of 10:00). This is the single fastest way to lose a user's trust and their workout. | Make Stop a hold-to-confirm (press-and-hold ~800 ms with a radial fill on the ring) or show a one-tap sheet: "End workout? / Resume". When ended early via Stop, title the screen "Stopped" with an amber icon, show elapsed vs planned ("0:19 of 10:00"), and render the bar at its true fill, not 100% green. |
| 2 | Critical | 18 | Landscape home overflows by 175 px: the TABATA row is clipped and the settings gear collides with the cut-off region; the layout does not scroll. | In a supported orientation (there is even a "Landscape only" setting on 09), a quarter of the app's core content is unreachable. A garage-gym user who props the phone landscape and locks it there cannot start a Tabata from home. This is the definition of landscape-as-afterthought. | Stopgap: wrap the landscape home in a scroll view. Proper fix: a 2×2 mode grid in landscape (each cell ≥ 160 pt tall, tick + name + subtitle), gear moved to top-right. Test on iPhone 16 Pro Max landscape with no debug stripes and all four modes + gear visible. |
| 3 | High | 10, 11, 12, 15, 16, 20 | Workout phase is not glanceable at 3 m. Work vs rest vs get-ready vs paused is encoded only in a ~14 pt pill caption and a 4 px progress bar; the giant digits stay identical white in every phase (16 REST and 11 WORK differ only in tiny blue vs green pill text; 12 PAUSED digits look exactly like running digits). | The app's stated wedge is "gym-visible UI", and the one thing a Tabata athlete needs from across the room is "am I working or resting". At distance, in a dim garage, every phase currently looks the same. A silent pause (pocket tap) is undetectable mid-WOD. | Colour the giant digits (or flood a 10–15% background wash) per phase: work = green, rest = blue, get-ready = orange, paused = 40% grey with a slow 1 Hz blink. Keep the pill label as the redundant text cue for colour-blind users. Acceptance test: phase identifiable in a 1-second glance from 3 m in a dim room. |
| 4 | High | 13, 11 | AMRAP's completion stat is "TOTAL TIME", and mid-workout there is no round count or way to log one. For a full AMRAP the total time is a constant the athlete chose (10:00): the app returns their own input as the "result". Home (01) even promises "Max rounds in time". | The AMRAP athlete's score IS rounds. After every AMRAP this screen hands them a number they already knew, and the thing they staggered over to see does not exist. That is the moment that decides whether the app survives next session against the wall clock plus whiteboard. | Add a tap-anywhere round counter during AMRAP (any tap on the dead centre area = +1, show "R7" at ≥ 40 pt under the digits), then make ROUNDS the hero stat on 13 with time secondary. If no feature can be added, at minimum relabel the stat honestly ("DURATION 10:00") so it doesn't pose as a result. |
| 5 | High | 01, 03, 06, 10, 14, 16 | The colour system collides: home teaches mode hues (AMRAP green, For Time blue, EMOM magenta, Tabata orange), but runtime reuses the same hues as phase colours (work green, rest blue, prep orange). So "FOR TIME · WORK" renders green not blue (14), "TABATA · REST" renders For-Time blue (16), an AMRAP prep pill renders Tabata orange (10), Tabata setup marks WORK with AMRAP's green dot (06), and EMOM's magenta never appears again after 01. Brand green additionally means selected, toggles, START, Done, and pause. | One hue carrying two meanings is a broken system, and this one has three hues doing double duty plus one orphan. Anyone who internalises the home colour coding is actively mistaught by every active screen. It also reads as unfinished craft to a design-literate buyer. | Give each hue exactly one owner. Recommended: phase colours own runtime (green/blue/orange/grey), and home mode ticks go monochrome (white or brand green for all four). Alternative: keep mode hues but shift phases to non-colliding hues (rest cyan, prep amber) and retire magenta. Audit all 21 screens so no hue means two things. |

## 2. Full findings

### Home (01, 18)

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| F1 | High | 01, 02–06, 08 | The voice-cue wedge is invisible before the first workout: nothing on home or any setup screen mentions voices; the only surface is a Settings row ("Voice: Major") behind a dim bottom-centre gear. | The differentiator versus every free timer is recorded coach voices, and a first-run user can complete their entire first workout without knowing it exists or who will suddenly start shouting. | Add a voice chip to every setup screen directly above START (speaker icon + "Voice: Major", tap opens the 08 sheet). Optionally a one-line caption under START: "Voice cues by Major". |
| F2 | Medium | 01 | Vertical composition clumps: wordmark at top, all four mode strips packed in the lower-middle, ~400 px of raw void between logo and list, gear floating alone at bottom. | The first screen sets the quality bar. Right now the layout reads as an unstyled column with leftover space, not a composed screen, and it wastes the chance to make the four modes feel like the app's four big buttons. | Distribute the four strips across the full height between wordmark and gear (equal vertical rhythm), or vertically centre the strip group as a block. Each strip becomes a bounded card row ≥ 88 pt tall so the tap area is visible. |
| F3 | Low | 01 | No shared left grid: the "WOD." wordmark, the colour ticks, and the mode text sit on three different x positions; the row chevrons are near-invisible grey. | Misaligned left edges on a five-element screen undermine the premium feel the type is working for; invisible chevrons weaken the "these are tappable" signal. | Align wordmark and tick bars to one 24 pt margin; raise chevron contrast to ≥ 3:1 or drop chevrons and rely on card affordance. |
| F4 | Medium | 18 | Beyond the overflow bug (Top-5 #2), landscape home is a stretched portrait column: full-width strips with chevrons stranded ~90% of the screen away from the labels, right half of the screen empty. | Even once it fits, this is not a designed landscape screen; eye travel from label to chevron is absurd and the layout signals "afterthought", contradicting the explicit-landscape promise. | Same fix as Top-5 #2: purpose-built 2×2 grid, content-width cells, no full-width chevron rows. |

### Setup screens (02, 03, 04, 05, 06, 19)

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| F5 | High | 02–06 | The −/+ steppers, the primary setup controls, are drawn with ~1.3–2:1 contrast outlines and glyphs; they read as disabled. Non-text controls need ≥ 3:1 (WCAG 1.4.11). | The 15-second-setup bar depends on instantly spotting the controls; ghost-outline buttons that look inactive make a sweaty athlete hesitate, and in a dim garage they may genuinely not see them. | Raise stepper borders and glyphs to ≥ 3:1 (e.g. #8A8A99 on #050510) and give them a 6–8% fill so they read as buttons. Verify with a contrast checker on the actual build. |
| F6 | Medium | 02, 05, 06 | Three different stepper grammars: duration rows put −/+ around the text labels "MIN"/"SEC" with the value far above (02); rounds puts −/+ directly around the value (05); Tabata adds a "5s" step caption between −/+ that no other stepper has (06). | One control, three layouts. It slows learning, and the "MIN − +" arrangement makes it genuinely ambiguous which button edits minutes vs seconds on first use. Only Tabata tells you the step size; AMRAP/For Time/EMOM steps are a mystery until tapped. | Unify on the Tabata pattern: value with unit, −/+ flanking it, step caption shown on all steppers ("1m", "15s"). Same component, same spacing, all five setup surfaces. |
| F7 | Medium | 02, 03, 05, 06 | The summary card's TOTAL DURATION silently includes the fixed 10 s prep: a 10:00 AMRAP reads "10m 10s", 8×(20+10) Tabata reads "4m 10s". Nothing explains the extra 10 s. Completion screens then exclude prep (17: 2 rounds of 30 s reads "01:00"). | To a first-time user "10m 10s" for a 10:00 AMRAP looks like an off-by-one bug, and the definition of "total" flips between setup and results. Small, but it is a trust leak on the very numbers the app exists to get right. | Show the workout duration only ("10m"), with a secondary caption "+ 0:10 get-ready". Keep setup and completion using the same definition. |
| F8 | Low | 02, 03, 05, 06 | The summary card restates the screen: "TYPE: AMRAP" inside the AMRAP screen titled AMRAP, duplicating the values just set above. On 02/03 the card adds no information at all beyond the +10 s. | Redundant furniture pushes the card toward template-y; the space could carry the voice chip (F1) or round/interval maths that actually helps ("10 × 1:00"). | Drop the TYPE row entirely; keep the card only where it computes something (EMOM/Tabata totals), and anchor it directly above START to kill the dead gap (F9). |
| F9 | Low | 02, 03 | Content clusters at the top with a ~600 px void between summary card and START; the pinned START is right, but the middle of the screen is unresolved. | The setup screens feel two-thirds empty in portrait, which reads as unfinished rather than minimal; on 06 the same template is suddenly full, so the family has no consistent rhythm. | Vertically centre the picker cluster or anchor card + START as one bottom group with a fixed 24 pt gap; apply the same rule to all four setups. |
| F10 | Medium | 03, 04, 14 | COUNT UP / COUNT DOWN changes what the giant runtime number means, but at runtime the only cue is a ~14 pt grey "Elapsed" floating 330 px below the digits (14); the unselected chip is a ghost outline that barely reads as an option. | Mid-workout, "00:09" could be nine seconds in or nine remaining near a cap; the disambiguating label is unreadable from any distance and detached from the number it describes. | Move the direction label to within 24 px of the digits at ≥ 20 pt ("ELAPSED" / "REMAINING" in the phase colour), and give the unselected chip a visible border (≥ 3:1). |
| F11 | Medium | 06 | The CLASSIC TABATA chip's affordance is ambiguous: it is styled like a selected state (green border, tinted fill) but sits alone at the top; nothing indicates whether it is a button, a badge, or what happens if the pickers below are edited (does it deselect?). | The densest setup screen leads with a component whose behaviour cannot be predicted, and it is the closest thing the app has to a preset, so it will be tapped. | Make it a real control: add a trailing state ("Applied ✓" vs "Tap to apply"), and visually deselect it the moment any picker deviates from 20/10×8. |
| F12 | Low | 19, 02 | Landscape START is labelled "START" while portrait says "START WORKOUT"; the two-column landscape setup otherwise works, but the left picker column's bottom half is empty while the right column is packed. | Same action, two names, for no reason; column weight imbalance makes the screen feel tilted. | Use one label ("START") in both orientations; vertically centre the picker cluster within the left column. |

## 3. Three pointed questions

- **What, if anything, would make you not keep using this app?** After my first AMRAP it showed me "TOTAL TIME 10:00", a number I typed in myself ten minutes earlier, with no rounds and no way to have counted them (13, 11); the wall clock plus chalk on the floor beats that, and with no history either, nothing I do in this app survives to tomorrow.
- **What would make you stop trusting it mid-workout?** One accidental tap on the red square while reaching for Pause killing the whole session and being congratulated with "Finished!" and a 100% green bar at 0:19 of 10:00 (12→13); secondarily, glancing from the bar during Tabata and being unable to tell rest from work because the giant digits look identical in every phase (11 vs 16).
- **If you could ship only one change before the next release, what is it and why?** Protect and honestly report the Stop action (hold-to-stop or confirm, plus a true "Stopped" completion state): it is the only finding that destroys a user's workout outright, every other issue costs seconds or polish, this one costs the artifact the athlete came for.

## 4. One genuine strength

The giant w900 Outfit timer digits on pure ink (11, 15, 20) are genuinely readable from across a gym and give the app a distinctive typographic identity; whatever changes, do not shrink or dilute that clock.
