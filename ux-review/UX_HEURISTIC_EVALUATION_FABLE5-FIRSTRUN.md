# UX Heuristic Evaluation — The Wharf WOD Timer

**Reviewer:** FABLE5-FIRSTRUN (cold, independent review; evidence = the 21 PNGs in `screens/` + `REVIEW_REQUEST.md` only)
**Primary lens:** the skeptical first-time user — 5-second test, 15-second setup, first-contact comprehension of AMRAP / For Time / EMOM / Tabata, zero-onboarding orientation, and discoverability of the voice-cue wedge before the first workout.

**Capture-set note:** the brief's screen table lists `17_active_tabata_phase_preview.png`, but that file is not in `screens/` (only `17_active_tabata_complete.png` exists). The "WORK in Ns" phase preview is therefore **unevaluated**, and the brief has two rows numbered 17.

## 1. Top 5 problems, ranked

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Critical | 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16 | **Ending a workout is booby-trapped.** A single tap on Stop instantly and irreversibly ends the session and lands on a green check + "Finished!" (13) — even at 0:19 of a 10:00 AMRAP. There is no confirmation, no undo, no resume. In For Time (14) it's worse: the *real* finish action is a grey flag that uses the same dim treatment the app uses for recessive controls, so it reads as disabled, and two adjacent buttons both end the workout with no visible difference in outcome. | The moment of use is panic-adjacent: dropped barbell, dog, phone call, or a first-run user just poking Stop "to get out". One mis-tap at 170 bpm destroys the WOD's timing forever, and the app then *lies* about it ("Finished!"). For a first-time user testing the app, this is the very first end-of-loop impression: the app celebrates a workout that didn't happen. Trust is gone in one tap. | (a) Make Stop **hold-to-end** (~600 ms ring-fill) with tap showing a "Hold to end" hint; (b) or route Stop → paused state with "End workout?" Resume / End choice. (c) On an early end, title the screen **"Stopped"** (no check), show "0:19 of 10:00" so the record is honest. (d) In For Time, replace the flag with a full-width **FINISH** bar (see #4 in full findings) so "log my time" and "abort" can never be confused. Testable: mis-tap Stop mid-WOD → timing recoverable within 2 taps; early-stop screen never says "Finished!". |
| 2 | Critical | 18 (+09) | **Landscape home is broken: TABATA is unreachable.** The landscape home renders portrait-proportioned rows without scrolling and overflows by 175 px — EMOM is half-clipped, TABATA is entirely cut off, and the settings gear floats between rows. The brief confirms the clipped content is real in release. The app itself ships a "Landscape only" orientation lock (09), so a garage-gym user who sets it can never start a Tabata from home. | This isn't polish — a whole mode disappears in exactly the propped-phone orientation the app claims as a differentiator ("explicit landscape layouts"). A first-run user who rotates on the first screen sees a broken app; a landscape-locked user loses 25% of the product. | Rebuild landscape home as a **2×2 grid** of the four mode cards (they fit comfortably in 2868×1320) with the gear pinned to a corner; as a stopgap, wrap the list in a scroll view. Testable: on iPhone 16 Pro Max landscape, all four modes + gear visible and tappable with no overflow indicator in debug. |
| 3 | High | 12, 15, 16 (also 11, 20) | **Mid-workout state and phase are only signalled by small top-pill text; the giant digits never change.** Tabata work vs rest (16): white digits in both phases, only the 14-pt-ish letterspaced pill ("TABATA · REST") and the thin bar turn blue. Paused (12) looks identical to running from any distance — white 09:41, tiny grey pill, small play icon. EMOM/Tabata round counters ("Round 2/10", 15; "Round 1/2", 16) are small grey text, unreadable from 3 m. | The app's own bar is 3-metre glanceability, and the *only* element that survives 3 m is the giant timer — which is state-blind. An athlete glancing between reps can't tell work from rest; someone whose phone got paused (pocket tap, accidental brush) keeps working against a stopped clock and the WOD is ruined without them knowing. The round count is the second most important number in EMOM/Tabata and it's the least visible thing on screen. | Colour the **digits themselves** by phase (work = green, rest = blue, prep = orange) or flood a full-screen background tint; when paused, dim digits to ~40% and pulse them (grey pill alone is not enough). Promote "Round 2/10" to ≥64 pt white directly under the timer — the screen is ~80% empty. Testable: from 3 m in a dim room, a viewer names phase (work/rest/paused) and round without reading any text smaller than the round counter. |
| 4 | High | 11, 13 | **AMRAP — the first-listed, flagship mode — gives the athlete nothing back.** The running screen (11) has no way to count rounds, and the completion screen (13) shows only TOTAL TIME, which for any completed AMRAP is just the duration the user configured 10 minutes ago (and after an early Stop, an arbitrary elapsed number). The one thing an AMRAP athlete staggers over to see — rounds (+ reps) completed — does not exist anywhere in the loop. | The brief's own success criterion: "after the final rep they want exactly one thing: their time/rounds." For AMRAP, that thing is rounds, and this app can't tell them. The free alternatives they're skeptical against (SmartWOD-style apps) have tap-to-count; the wall clock plus chalk does it too. This is the difference between "keep it installed" and "cute timer, deleted". | Add a **tap-anywhere-to-count-round** interaction on the AMRAP running screen (entire empty canvas = the target; show "ROUNDS 4" large under the timer), and put ROUNDS as the primary card on the AMRAP completion screen with TOTAL TIME secondary. Testable: sweaty-finger round increments from 2–3 m posture (big target), and the completion screen leads with rounds. |
| 5 | High | 01, 02–06, 07, 08 | **The #1 differentiator — recorded voice cues — is invisible until it starts talking.** Home (01) has zero mention of voice. All four setup screens (02–06) show duration/rounds/summary/START — no voice indicator. The only surface is a "Voice — Major >" row behind the small bottom gear (07), and the picker (08) has **no preview/play button**, no "Off" option, "Holly" gets no descriptor (Major and Liam do), and the three named voices share inconsistent icons. A first-time user cannot discover, audition, or opt out of the wedge before their first workout. | The skeptical first-run user decides in the setup flow whether this beats the wall clock. Nothing on screens 01–06 gives them a reason. Worse, the first impression of the wedge is whichever voice happens to be default, unauditioned; if they'd have loved Liam but got Major, the feature landed flat. And with no visible Off, users who *don't* want a voice can't see how to silence it (Sound Effects is a separate toggle). | Put a **voice chip on every setup screen** next to START — e.g. `🔊 Major ▸` — that opens the picker; add a **play-preview button per voice** in the picker (08) playing a 1–2 s "3-2-1 GO!" sample; add an **Off** option; give Holly a descriptor. Testable: a new user, without entering Settings, can name the three voices and has heard one before ever pressing START WORKOUT. |

## 2. Full findings

### Screen 01 — Home

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| 6 | Medium | 01 | The hero is "WOD." and nothing else; ~45% of the screen between the logo and the first mode row is empty, and the app's promise (voice-coached, gym-visible) appears nowhere. The 5-second test passes for *what it is* (a WOD timer with 4 modes — the subtitles "Max rounds in time", "Race the clock" etc. are genuinely good) but fails for *why this one*. | A skeptic granting 5 seconds sees a generic mode list they could get from any free timer; the differentiators never get their one shot. | Use one line of the dead space: a subtitle under "WOD." — e.g. "Voice-coached gym timer" — and/or a small voice chip (see finding 5). Keep the rest empty. |
| 7 | Low | 01 | Settings gear is a small, low-contrast grey icon alone at the bottom; row chevrons are so dim they barely register. | First-run users hunting for voices/orientation may not notice the only door to Settings; weak affordances on an otherwise sparse screen. | Raise gear + chevron contrast to ≥3:1 against #050510; give the gear a 44-pt visible touch shape. |
| 8 | Low | 01, 03, 06 | Mode-name casing flips between surfaces: home says "FOR TIME"/"TABATA", setup titles say "For Time"/"Tabata", active pills return to all-caps. Button labels also flip: "START WORKOUT" (02–06) vs "START" (19). | Small, but it's the kind of unevenness a first-run user subconsciously reads as "unfinished". | Pick one casing per surface type and one START label; apply everywhere. |

### Screens 02–06 — Setup flow (all modes)

| # | Severity | Screen(s) | Issue | Why it matters | Suggested fix |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|----------------|---------------|

EMOM 10×1:00 → "10m 10s" (05). Classic Tabata — which every CrossFitter knows is exactly 4:00 — reads "4m 10s" (06). The hidden +10 s is the prep countdown, but no screen ever says so. Then the completion screen *excludes* prep ("TOTAL TIME 01:00" for 2×(20+10) on 17), so setup and result never reconcile. | This is the first number a knowledgeable athlete fact-checks, and it looks wrong on first contact ("Tabata is 4 minutes, why does it say 4m 10s?"). Unexplained numbers at setup + inconsistent accounting at completion = trust erosion in an app whose whole job is counting time. | Show the configured work time as the summary ("4m 00s") and list prep separately: "+ 10s get-ready". | 10 | Medium | 02, 03, 05 | Seconds/minutes steppers have **no step-size label** (Tabata's work/rest steppers do show "5s" — the one place it's done right), no visible alternative to tap-tap-tap, and no indication whether press-and-hold repeats. | 11 | Medium | 02–06 | Stepper buttons and their hairline outlines are extremely low contrast (dim grey on near-black) — the same visual weight this app uses elsewhere for recessive/disabled-looking controls. | In a dim garage with sweaty hands, the primary editing controls of the whole app are the hardest things on the screen to see. | Raise stepper outline/glyph contrast to ≥3:1; on press, flash the affected digits of the big number so the mapping (MIN± → left pair) is taught by feedback. |
| 12 | Medium | 06 | The CLASSIC TABATA chip is ambiguous: is it a tappable preset (resets to 20/10×8) or a status badge saying "current config = classic"? It has a border like a button and an icon like a label. What happens to it when the user changes work to 40 s is unknowable. | The densest setup screen leads with an element whose behaviour can't be predicted before tapping — a first-contact comprehension miss on the exact screen the 40/20×7 benchmark runs through. | Make it explicitly a preset button ("Reset to classic 20/10 × 8") that visibly deactivates (or disappears) once values diverge. |
| 13 | Low | 03, 04 | "TIME CAP … TOTAL DURATION 20m 10s" mislabels For Time: the cap is a ceiling, not a duration — the workout ends when the athlete finishes. The COUNT UP/DOWN control itself is clear (04 shows a clean selected state), but nothing says what happens at the cap. | Athletes know caps; calling a cap "total duration" misteaches the app's own model of the mode and feeds finding 9's mistrust. | Label the summary "TIME CAP 20m" for For Time; one caption line: "Timer stops at cap." |
| 14 | Low | 02–06 | The SUMMARY card mostly repeats what's already on screen (TYPE "AMRAP" under an "AMRAP" title; the same duration twice in two formats) while consuming prime mid-screen space. | Redundant info costs a glance every setup, and the space could carry something new (voice chip, finding 5). | Cut TYPE; keep only derived values the pickers don't show (total duration, and per finding 9, the prep line). |

## 3. Three pointed questions

**What, if anything, would make you not keep using this app?**
AMRAP is the mode the app lists first and the one I run most, and it gives me nothing the gym wall clock doesn't: I can't count rounds during it (11), and at the end it hands me back the number I typed in ten minutes earlier (13). Add finding 9's "4m 10s Tabata" and I'd conclude the app doesn't understand my workouts — SmartWOD-style free apps count rounds, so I'd quietly switch back after the novelty of the voice wears off.

**What would make you stop trusting it mid-workout?**
One tap on Stop — accidental, or forced by a dropped bar — permanently killing the session and being greeted with a cheerful check mark and "Finished!" (13). Second place: glancing over mid-Tabata and being unable to tell work from rest, or that the timer had silently been sitting in a paused state that looks exactly like running (12, 16). A timer app has exactly one promise; both of these break it.

**If you could ship only one change before the next release, what is it and why?**
Fix the end-of-workout flow (Top-5 #1): hold-to-end on Stop, an honest "Stopped — 0:19 of 10:00" state with Resume, and a real FINISH bar for For Time. It is the single change that touches every mode, protects the athlete's data at the exact panic moment the brief describes, removes the app's one outright lie, and it is the first flaw every curious first-run user will trip over when they poke Stop to exit their test run. (The landscape overflow, Top-5 #2, is the mandatory bug-fix that rides along in the same release.)

## 4. One genuine strength

The in-workout hero typography (11, 15, 20) — white Outfit w900 digits at enormous size on deep ink — is authentically readable from 3 metres at a glance, which is the core promise, and it should be treated as untouchable while everything around it is fixed.
